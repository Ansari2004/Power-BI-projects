# Freelancing Earnings Power BI Project

## Overview
This Power BI project visualizes freelancing earnings data, providing insights into earnings by job category, client region, experience level, project type, platform, and more. The dashboard includes various charts to help analyze trends and patterns in freelancing data.

## Features
- **Sum of Earnings by Job Category**: Bar chart showing total earnings (in USD) for categories like Graphic Design, App Development, etc.
- **Count of Job Category by Client Region**: Pie chart displaying the distribution of job categories across client regions (e.g., USA, Australia, Europe).
- **Sum of Hourly Rate by Experience Level**: Area chart comparing hourly rates for Beginner, Intermediate, and Expert levels.
- **Sum of Client Rating by Experience Level**: Pie chart showing client ratings distribution by experience level.
- **Sum of Marketing Spend by Experience Level**: Area chart illustrating marketing spend across experience levels.
- **Sum of Rehire Rate by Experience Level**: Pie chart detailing rehire rates by experience level.
- **Sum of Rehire Rate by Project Type**: Pie chart comparing rehire rates for Fixed and Hourly projects.
- **Sum of Rehire Rate by Client Rating**: Line chart showing rehire rates based on client ratings.
- **Sum of Rehire Rate and Job Success Rate by Job Duration**: Line chart analyzing rehire and success rates over job duration in days.
- **Sum of Marketing Spend by Platform**: Area chart showing marketing spend across platforms like Upwork, Toptal, etc.
- **Sum of Hourly Rate by Platform**: Line chart comparing hourly rates across different freelancing platforms.

## Data Source
The data used in this project is sourced from freelancing platforms and includes metrics such as earnings, client ratings, rehire rates, and job durations.

## How to Use
1. Open the Power BI file (`Freelancing_Earnings.pbix`) in Power BI Desktop.
2. Explore the interactive dashboards and filters to analyze the data.
3. Use the visuals to identify trends and make data-driven decisions.

## Installation
- Download and install [Power BI Desktop](https://powerbi.microsoft.com/desktop/).
- Clone or download this repository.
- Open the `.pbix` file to view the dashboard.
